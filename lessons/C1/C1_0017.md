# Populism and Nationalism

**Level:** C1 - Advanced  
**Lesson ID:** C1_0017  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Methodology:** Communicative Language Teaching (CLT) with Task-Based Learning

## Discussion Questions

1. How would you characterize the discourse surrounding populism and nationalism?
2. What underlying assumptions shape our understanding of populism and nationalism?
3. To what extent is populism and nationalism a product of social construction?
4. How do power dynamics influence populism and nationalism?
5. What are the epistemological challenges in studying populism and nationalism?
6. How might interdisciplinary approaches enhance our grasp of populism and nationalism?
7. What are the implications of populism and nationalism for policy formulation?
8. How does populism and nationalism intersect with issues of equity and justice?
9. What paradigm shifts have occurred in thinking about populism and nationalism?
10. How can we reconcile competing frameworks for understanding populism and nationalism?
11. What are the long-term ramifications of current trends in populism and nationalism?
12. How does populism and nationalism challenge conventional wisdom?

## Useful Expressions


### Academic Discourse

- It is worth noting that...
- One might contend that...
- This raises the question of...
- It is incumbent upon us to consider...

### Nuanced Argument

- While it is true that..., one must also consider...
- This is not to say that..., rather...
- The extent to which... remains debatable
- This phenomenon can be attributed to...

### Synthesis

- Drawing these threads together...
- In synthesizing these perspectives...
- The convergence of these factors suggests...
- Taking a holistic view...
