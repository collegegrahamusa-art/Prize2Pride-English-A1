# Volunteering

**Level:** B1 | **Category:** social  
**Learning Style:** Visual Learners  
**Cognitive Level:** Intermediate/Application  
**Lesson ID:** B1_social_visual_intermediate_0009  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes

---

## Learning Objectives

- Apply learned vocabulary in conversations about volunteering
- Compare different perspectives on volunteering
- Analyze simple arguments and opinions

## Lesson Structure

### Warm-Up Activity

**Activity:** Image Analysis  
**Description:** Students examine images related to volunteering and discuss  
**Materials:** Images, photos, or video clips

### Main Activities


#### Activity 1: Input Activity

- **Learning Style Adaptation:** Use of diagrams, charts, and mind maps
- **Cognitive Level:** Apply
- **Description:** Students receive information about volunteering through visual modality
- **Time:** 10-15 minutes


#### Activity 2: Practice Activity

- **Learning Style Adaptation:** Color-coding and highlighting
- **Cognitive Level:** Analyze
- **Description:** Students practice language related to volunteering using visual approach
- **Time:** 15-20 minutes


#### Activity 3: Production Activity

- **Learning Style Adaptation:** Visual organizers and infographics
- **Cognitive Level:** Apply language in new contexts
- **Description:** Students produce original content about volunteering demonstrating intermediate thinking
- **Time:** 15-20 minutes

### Discussion Prompts

1. How does volunteering affect people?
2. What are the advantages and disadvantages of volunteering?
3. How would you solve problems related to volunteering?
