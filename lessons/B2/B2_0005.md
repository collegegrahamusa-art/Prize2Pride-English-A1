# Mental Health and Wellbeing

**Level:** B2 - Upper Intermediate  
**Lesson ID:** B2_0005  
**Duration:** 45-60 minutes  
**Methodology:** Communicative Language Teaching (CLT) with Task-Based Learning

---

## Learning Objectives

- Students will analyze and evaluate different perspectives on mental health and wellbeing
- Students will articulate complex ideas with supporting arguments
- Students will demonstrate spontaneous interaction with minimal hesitation
- Students will use advanced discourse markers to structure their speech

---

## Can-Do Statements (CEFR)

- Understand main ideas of complex text on concrete and abstract topics
- Interact with degree of fluency and spontaneity with native speakers

---

- Passive voice (all tenses)
- Reported speech

## Warm-Up Activity

**Activity:** Brainstorming and Categorizing  
**Time:** 7-10 minutes

**Description:** Students brainstorm ideas related to mental health and wellbeing and categorize them

**Instructions:**

1. Write the topic on the board
1. Students call out related words and concepts
1. Group ideas into categories
1. Discuss connections between categories

## Discussion Questions

1. To what extent do you agree that mental health and wellbeing is important?
2. What are the main challenges associated with mental health and wellbeing?
3. How might mental health and wellbeing evolve in the next decade?
4. What factors influence people's attitudes toward mental health and wellbeing?
5. In what ways does mental health and wellbeing affect society?
6. What measures could be taken to improve mental health and wellbeing?
7. How does mental health and wellbeing differ across cultures?
8. What role does government play in mental health and wellbeing?
9. What are the ethical implications of mental health and wellbeing?
10. How can individuals contribute to mental health and wellbeing?

## Homework Assignment

**Task:** Write a 250-word opinion piece on mental health and wellbeing

**Requirements:**

- Present a clear argument with supporting evidence
- Use discourse markers to structure your writing
- Include counter-arguments and rebuttals

**Submission:** Submit before next class

## Assessment Criteria

- **Fluency:** Can express ideas smoothly with few pauses
- **Accuracy:** Shows good grammatical control with few errors
- **Vocabulary:** Has good range of vocabulary with some precision
- **Pronunciation:** Clear and natural with good control
- **Interaction:** Can engage actively and maintain discourse
